📈 24H Outlook — How It's Calculated
Each stock card shows a plain-English 24-hour price prediction. It uses a deterministic 6-component model — no random numbers:
  1. Net Score → primary direction driver (±ATR-scaled)
  2. RSI correction → overbought pushes target down; oversold pushes it up
  3. MACD momentum → aligned MACD adds confidence; opposing MACD reduces target
  4. EMA20 gravity → price gravitates back toward its 20-day moving average
  5. Volume conviction → 1.5× volume confirms the move; <0.6× discounts it
  6. ATR budget → if today already used its full daily range, less room remains
The range (e.g. −0.4% to +3.2%) is the ±ATR confidence band around the central estimate. The pattern label (Momentum / Reversal snap / Gap fade / Steady / Choppy) describes how the move is expected to unfold during the session.
Scoring formula
Net Score = BullishScore + CatalystScore − BearishScore
Conviction % = 6-factor weighted score (0–99)
Bullish triggers: RSI dip zone · MACD rising · price ≥ EMA20 · near week low · rel. strength vs SPY
Catalyst bonus: insider buying · selling exhaustion · earnings proximity · volume breakout · mid-range momentum breakout · market tailwind
Bearish triggers: RSI overbought · below EMA20 · near week high · insider selling · market headwind · lagging SPY
Badge is shown first — stocks sorted by badge tier (🟢 Buy Now first), then by net score.
